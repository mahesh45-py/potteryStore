DIY Crafts for Kids: Decorative Clay Diyas for Diwali

Team iDiva


Clay diyas to brighten up your Diwali

Diwali is the festival of lights; thus, the brighter the better. Behold this motto, as we've decided to add some eco-friendly lights to your abode with this do-it-yourself diyas. If your too caught up with all the festive preps and want your kids to be busy too, sit them down to make these super simple clay diyas that will not make them feel crafty, but will also make a useful item to decorate your homes for Diwali. So, get a box full of colourful clay and put your little angels to work with these do-it-yourself diyas.

What you need:

A box of colourful clay
An empty pen refill
A pen

Method:

1. Using the palm of your hand, make a one-inch ball of the clay (orange colour).

2. Using your thumb finger, make a small cavity in the ball. Evenly press the clay from the sides to give it the shape of a diya.

3. Use a piece of card paper to make small cuts in the inside of the lamp to hold the thread.

4. Now using the palm of both your hands, roll a piece of white clay into a thread. Place the thread inside the diya.

5. Take a piece of green clay and roll it into a thin thread.

6. Pick the thin thread from the centre. Using your fingers roll it from the closed end by which it will look like a rope. Place this on the rim of the diya.

7. Make very small balls of different colors, flatten them and place them on the outside of the diya.

8. You can use your own creativity to add to the beauty of the diya.

9. Use the pen and refill to make designs on the diya.

10. Interestingly, by replacing the clay thread with a cotton thread and pouring oil in the diya, you can use it as a regular diya.
